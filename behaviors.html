Welcome to Rooted Parenting. This app is designed to help parents understand behavior, respond with calm, and build emotional safety at home.

Choose what is happening right now:

Tantrums / Emotional Overload

What is happening: The child is overwhelmed and does not yet have the skills to regulate emotions. Their nervous system is in distress mode.

What to say:

  • "I can see this is really hard right now."
  • "You're not in trouble. I'm here to help."
  • "We can figure this out together."

Avoid saying:

  • "Stop crying right now."
  • "You're being dramatic."
  • "If you don't stop..."

Support steps:

  • Stay calm
  • Reduce stimulation
  • Offer presence without pressure
  • Talk after child is calm

Defiance / Not Listening

What is happening: The child may feel powerless, unheard, or overwhelmed. Control can feel like safety.

What to say:

  • "Let's figure this out together."
  • "You have two choices."
  • "I will help you succeed."

Avoid saying:

  • "Because I said so."
  • "You never listen."

Support steps:

  • Give simple choices
  • Use calm voice
  • Break task into steps

Anxiety / Worry

What is happening: The child's brain is trying to stay safe. Their body may feel tense or overwhelmed.

What to say:

  • "You are safe right now."
  • "We can take this one step at a time."
  • "I believe in you."

Support steps:

  • slow breathing together
  • break task into small steps
  • offer reassurance

Aggression / Hitting

What is happening: The child does not yet have safe ways to express anger or frustration.

What to say:

  • "I will not let you hurt others."
  • "You are safe. I am here."
  • "Let's find a safer way."

Support steps:

  • Block unsafe behavior calmly
  • Offer safe alternatives
  • Teach feeling words

Lying

What is happening: Lying often happens when children fear punishment or feel unsafe telling the truth.

What to say:

  • "You can tell me the truth. You are safe."
  • "Mistakes help us learn."

Avoid saying:

  • "You're a liar."
  • "I can't trust you."

Support steps:

  • Reduce shame
  • Praise honesty
  • Focus on problem solving

Emotional Shutdown

What is happening: Child feels overwhelmed and disconnects as a protection response.

What to say:

  • "I'm here when you're ready."
  • "You don't have to talk yet."

Support steps:

  • reduce pressure
  • offer quiet presence
  • allow processing time

School Refusal

What is happening: Child may feel unsafe, overwhelmed, or worried about expectations.

What to say:

  • "We will figure this out together."
  • "You don't have to handle this alone."

Support steps:

  • identify stressor
  • talk with school supports
  • create gradual plan
Back to top

Attention Seeking

What is happening: Connection need is not being met in a positive way.

What to say:

  • "I see you."
  • "Let's spend time together."

Support steps:

  • schedule connection time
  • notice positive behavior
  • reduce negative attention cycles
