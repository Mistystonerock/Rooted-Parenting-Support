Rooted Parenting

Court and CPS Referral Packet

A trauma-informed parent skill-building program designed for caregivers involved with juvenile courts, child welfare systems, school-based family supports, and community agencies.

Program Snapshot

Program purpose

Rooted Parenting helps caregivers understand behavior, reduce escalations, respond with calm structure, and strengthen emotional safety, consistency, and follow-through at home.

Recommended use

This program may be used for parent education, caregiver skill-building, family stabilization support, reunification support, diversion planning, and behavior-focused service plans.

Target population

  • Caregivers of children with behavioral, emotional, school, or family stress concerns
  • Families affected by trauma, chronic stress, conflict, shutdown, aggression, anxiety, or defiance
  • Parents needing structured education on calm limits, reinforcement, routines, and non-harmful consequences

Delivery options

  • Individual parent coaching
  • Small group parent education
  • Family support or home visiting enhancement
  • Court-ordered or CPS-referred parent education with documentation

Core program elements

  • 10 structured lessons focused on behavior, trauma, regulation, connection, routines, and caregiver follow-through
  • Parent-child practice activities and reflection prompts
  • Guidance on calm consequences, predictable structure, and healthy reinforcement systems
  • Printable worksheets, progress tracking, attendance documentation, and completion certificate

Referral Guidance

Appropriate referral reasons

  • Escalating parent-child conflict or repeated power struggles
  • Caregiver difficulty using consistent, safe, developmentally appropriate discipline
  • Need for parent education around structure, routines, reinforcement, and emotional regulation
  • Concerns related to aggression, shutdown, school refusal, anxiety, lying, or chronic defiance
  • Need for documented caregiver participation in a parenting skills intervention

Program fit notes

  • Best used when caregivers can participate in reflective learning and practical homework between sessions.
  • May be paired with therapy, case management, family preservation, reunification planning, or school supports.
  • Not a substitute for psychiatric, medical, substance use, domestic violence, or emergency crisis services.

Participation Standards

Program element Standard
Session length45 to 60 minutes per session
Program length10 sessions or lessons
Practice expectationsCaregiver completes reflection and at least one parent-child practice activity between sessions when assigned
DocumentationAttendance, progress notes, and completion status may be tracked by the facilitator or referring program
Completion recommendationAttend all required sessions or complete equivalent make-up work, participate in reflection, and demonstrate engagement with core parenting skills

Expected skill areas

  • Understanding behavior as communication
  • Using regulation before discipline
  • Setting predictable limits with calm follow-through
  • Using positive reinforcement, routines, and practical consequences
  • Reducing shaming, threats, and reactive escalation
