Rooted Parenting

Sample Court Order and Referral Language

Use this page to help courts, CPS units, diversion programs, schools, and providers describe Rooted Parenting in formal referral or service-plan language.

How to Use This Page

  • Adapt wording to local court, agency, probation, or CPS requirements.
  • Use completion standards that match your actual delivery process.
  • Keep language focused on caregiver education, participation, and skill-building.
  • Avoid promising clinical outcomes that the program does not formally measure.

Sample Court Order Language

Parent / caregiver shall enroll in and participate in Rooted Parenting, a trauma-informed parenting education and skill-building program, or another approved equivalent provider if directed by the Court. Parent / caregiver shall complete all required sessions, engage in assigned home practice, and comply with reasonable attendance and participation expectations established by the program. Proof of enrollment, attendance, progress, and completion shall be provided to the referring party, caseworker, probation officer, guardian ad litem, or the Court as requested.

Sample CPS / Service Plan Language

Caregiver will participate in Rooted Parenting to strengthen parenting skills related to calm response, emotional regulation, structure, positive reinforcement, predictable consequences, and child behavior support. Caregiver will attend scheduled sessions, complete assigned practice activities when possible, and demonstrate engagement with the program's parenting education goals. Progress will be reviewed through attendance records, facilitator feedback, and completion status.

Sample Diversion / Community Referral Language

Participant is referred to Rooted Parenting for structured caregiver education focused on child behavior, parent-child conflict reduction, emotional safety, and healthy discipline practices. Completion may include attendance, reflection, home practice, and receipt of a completion certificate when minimum requirements are met.

Suggested Completion Standard Language

Completion is defined as successful participation in the required number of sessions or lessons, meaningful engagement in program activities, and sufficient participation to demonstrate exposure to core parenting content and practical skill-building.
