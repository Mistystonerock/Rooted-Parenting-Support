Rooted Parenting Program Manual

Implementation Guide for Courts, CPS, Schools, and Family Programs

A practical manual for using Rooted Parenting as a structured caregiver skill-building intervention.

Program Overview

Rooted Parenting is a trauma-informed parent education and skill-building program. It is designed to help caregivers understand behavior through a regulation and relationship lens while also strengthening structure, limits, routines, reinforcement, and safe follow-through.

Program goals

  • Increase caregiver understanding of stress, trauma, and child behavior
  • Reduce reactive, shaming, or escalating parent-child interactions
  • Strengthen calm limits, predictable consequences, and positive reinforcement
  • Support family stability, emotional safety, and caregiver confidence

Recommended settings

  • Juvenile court diversion or family court referrals
  • CPS service plans or reunification support
  • School-based family support programs
  • Behavior-focused community family services

Delivery options

  • Individual sessions
  • Group sessions
  • Hybrid in-person and printable support model
  • Facilitated learning plus homework practice

Practice Model

Core practice principles

  • Behavior is approached as communication, stress response, skill gap, or unmet need before it is approached as defiance alone.
  • Regulation comes before extended teaching or correction.
  • Connection and accountability can happen together.
  • Consequences should be calm, predictable, brief, and related whenever possible.
  • Positive reinforcement, structure, and repetition are central parts of behavior change.

Position on punishment and rewards

Rooted Parenting does not rely on harsh punishment, fear, shame, or intimidation. The program teaches caregivers to use developmentally appropriate structure, routine, reinforcement, and non-harmful consequences. Rewards are framed as reinforcement of desired skills and effort. Consequences are framed as predictable follow-through that teaches responsibility without escalating threat.

Implementation Standards

Recommended session format

  • Check-in and attendance
  • Review of previous practice
  • Lesson content
  • Parent-child activity assignment
  • Reflection and next-step planning

Completion criteria

  • Attendance at required sessions or documented equivalent make-up work
  • Participation in reflection and skill practice
  • Engagement with lesson activities
  • Facilitator determines minimum completion standard has been met

Documentation tools

  • Referral packet
  • Pre/post parenting assessment
  • Attendance and progress report
  • Completion certificate
  • Facilitator guide and workbook

Facilitator Expectations

Facilitators should be able to

  • Use respectful, non-shaming, trauma-informed language
  • Support accountability without escalating threat
  • Teach predictable routines, reinforcement, and safe consequences
  • Recognize when higher-level clinical, crisis, or safety intervention is needed
  • Document participation and progress in a clear, professional way

Important limits of the program

Rooted Parenting is an educational and skill-building intervention. It is not a replacement for clinical treatment, emergency safety planning, psychiatric care, substance use treatment, domestic violence intervention, or formal diagnostic assessment.
