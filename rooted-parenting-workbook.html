Family Profile

How to Use This Workbook

  • Read one daily lesson at a time instead of trying to do everything at once.
  • Use the daily notes to track what happened, what worked, and what you want to practice tomorrow.
  • Focus on calm, connection, predictable follow-through, and repair.
  • When a child is highly upset, the brain often cannot take in a lecture or yelling. Calm first, teach second.

21-Day Learning Themes

This workbook follows the same 3-week structure used in the app.

Week 1

Safety and Regulation

  • Understand behavior as communication.
  • Notice stress brain versus thinking brain.
  • Practice calm first instead of yelling first.
  • Learn why fewer words often work better in upset moments.
Week 2

Connection and Follow-Through

  • Use connection before correction.
  • Practice positive praise for small steps.
  • Use calm consequences, redos, and predictable follow-through.
  • Reduce power struggles by being steady and clear.
Week 3

Habit Building and Long-Term Change

  • Build routines that support safety and success.
  • Track progress and repeat what helps.
  • Repair after hard moments instead of staying stuck in shame.
  • Finish the 21 days with a stronger parenting habit and certificate completion path.

A child in a highly upset state often cannot fully process lectures or yelling. Calm first, then teach the lesson when the brain is ready to hear it.

Connection Before Correction Examples

Example 1: Homework frustration

Connection first: “This feels hard right now. I’m with you.”

Correction after calm: “We still need to finish one small step before screens.”

Example 2: Disrespectful words

Connection first: “You sound really upset. Let’s get calm.”

Correction after calm: “You can be upset, but you still need a respectful redo.”

Example 3: Bedtime refusal

Connection first: “You wish you could stay up longer.”

Correction after calm: “Bedtime is still happening. Let’s choose the next step together.”
